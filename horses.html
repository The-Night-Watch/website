Free The Horses (2020)

3D animation

Free The
Horses

In the summer of 2016, I decided to make a 3D animation short film using Blender to teach myself 3D animation on a full project scale. The theme was chosen after a conversation with a friend regarding the moral implications of horse ownership.

The film itself took a couple of weeks to make, during which I learned a lot about 3D animation. The making of a 3D short film requires many different skills, some similar to real life filmmaking (frame composition, editing, sound design) while others are specific to 3D work (modeling, rigging, animation). I particularely enjoyed the filmmaking aspect of it, as directing in 3D offers infinite possibilities without the limitations of a real life film set.

The animation of the horse model took a good part of the time I had alloted to the project. The result is satisfying enough for the expectations I had set for myself, but the rig could still be considered basic compared to bigger budget productions. It was a great opportunity to learn about the creative and technical compromises necessary to adapt to time and resource constraints on this type of projects.
